Visualizing Temperature by Latitude

Using a scatterplot, we're able to visualize the relationship between humidity and latitude. Humidity is a measure of water vapor in the atmosphere. As air warms, it's able to hold more moisture and, thus be more humid. Given this, one can assume that the closer we are to the equator, the more humid the climate. The graph represents this well. We can see that the majority of the observations at the equator have humidity levels of 60% or more.
